## Overview

Polyanna (Player-ID tag `Polyanna`) is a native C64 tracker by Aleksi Eeben
(CNCD), released 28 January 2017 (V1.00) at Demobit's wild demo competition
(placed 2nd). Its distinguishing feature, per DeepSID's cached player spec,
is playing the SID's 3 regular voices together with 3 or 5 additional
emulated pulse-only channels (6 or 8 channels total) on a single, stock
6581 SID chip — reported elsewhere as the first 8-channel tracker for a
single-SID C64. It requires the older 6581 SID revision. 14 files in the
local dataset use it, spread across 11 different composers with no single
dominant user (the author himself contributed only 1 of the 14) — signal
of a small but genuinely used tool, not a personal routine, despite ranking
only #20 by file count among uncarded families in `knowledge/COVERAGE.md`.

## Quirks & gotchas

See the `quirks` array. The two load-bearing points: (1) the extra
emulated channels and the 6581-only requirement are the whole reason this
player is notable, but the exact playback technique (believed to be
volume-register-driven pulse emulation, in the spirit of other >3-voice SID
tricks) is not confirmed here by disassembly — only described secondhand by
DeepSID's spec and blog coverage; and (2) despite the same author having
also made Polly Tracker (2005) and John Player, and the superficially
similar name, no source or statement ties Polyanna's engine to either — the
author instead describes it as written from scratch in 3 days, so no
lineage `edges` are asserted.
